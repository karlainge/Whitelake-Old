Iron Age Period

This historic place has been dated back from the Iron Age between 1300BC-2BC. Iron is very strong in this specific place of the country where iron was traded all over the country and the continent e.g. France. In this period the city was speaking an old dialect of English called Brythonic Language, which is better known as Celtic. In the records there has was a castle on a hilltop watching over the city. Unfortunately some of the city got destroyed when word of the romans invaded and so the city was evacuated.

Roman Period

When the Romans came to the city, they had rebuild the parts which was destroyed from the Iron Age people. The Romans had built a road from the nearest city and also placed a fortress for an army for the north. The university was also built from the romans in the year 2AD. As the Romans had built the university, they had this idea of helping the town people by teaching them to read and write. With additional help of maintain the city itself. They had not stayed long as they were ordered to retreat from there campaign of ruling Europe and it came to the Roman downfall. With the result of the evacuation, some of the Romans stayed behind to live a new life from the Roman Empire.

Post-Medieval Period

After the English civil war Whitelake had to recover from what happened with all the fighting between the Parliamentarians and Royalists. Both William Cromwell, Oliver Cromwell's brother had fought here against the mighty and newly formed British Army. The newly formed army had William surrounded inside the tavern. He was arrested and taken to London where later he was hung for his crime against the crown. The army had protected the city till the Navy came in 1660. In the late 17th Century the James II of England had visited the city before the battle of Boyne had stayed there for a while to watch over the build of Whitelake Palace, unfortunately he could not see it finished.

Whitelake Palace

The Palace built in the late 17th century, with the overseeing of the King James II, King William who took the throne after the James II went to visit the palace on a numerous occasions up to his death on 1702. It has been kept in the royal family for generations and still being used present day. The beautiful palace had to be rebuilt due to the fall stone work it was causing and a small fire which was caused by a cat knocking a candle over. In the mid-18th century it was expanded to accommodate the changes, this was centred in the middle of the city next to a beautiful large river, with it placed in the centre of the old town it could see at 360 degree angle to if the old town was being attacked by enemy forces. During World War II it was close of being hit by an enemy bomb as the high ranking office of General Mark Wilson and his staff led the army forces from an underground cave. After the war the palace was reused as a home for the royal family and was opened for the public in the 1960's, with a few selected rooms only can be seen.
